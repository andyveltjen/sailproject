## Structure of the guide

The guidelines guide is structured around three levels:

### 1. Institution level
Frameworks for policymakers and heads of training:
- How do you formulate a future-proof AI policy?
- What governance structures are needed?
- How do you involve teachers and students in policy development?

### 2. Curriculum
Tools for curriculum and programme coordinators:
- Integrate AI literacy into final competences
- Adapt examination programmes
- Monitor coherence throughout the programme

### 3. Subject level
Concrete tools for individual teachers:
- Taxonomy of AI use by assignment type
- Sample formulations for assignments and test instructions
- Providing feedback on AI-assisted work

## First draft principles

Based on the literature review, we already formulate five guiding principles:

1. **Transparency** - Be clear about what is and is not allowed
2. **Purposefulness** - Always link AI use to explicit learning outcomes
3. **Critical thinking** - Deploy AI to stimulate higher-order thinking, not replace it
4. **Inclusiveness** - Take into account unequal access to AI tools
5. **Agility** - Build policies that can grow with rapidly evolving technology

*The guidelines will be further developed and validated in phases 2 and 3 of the study.*
