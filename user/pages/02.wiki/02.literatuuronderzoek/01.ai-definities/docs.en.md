## What is AI in education context?

In the education literature, the term "artificial intelligence" covers a broad spectrum of technologies. It is important to distinguish between different forms of AI relevant to education.

## Generative AI

Generative AI refers to systems that can produce new content - text, images, code - based on a prompt. The most prominent examples in educational contexts are:

- **Large Language Models (LLMs)**: ChatGPT (OpenAI), Claude (Anthropic), Gemini (Google)
- **Image generators**: DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion
- **Code assistants**: GitHub Copilot, Cursor

### Definition (Holmes et al., 2023)
> "Generative AI refers to AI systems that can generate novel content - text, images, audio, video, or code - that did not exist in their training data, by learning patterns from large datasets."

## AI literacy

Cardona et al (2023) identify four dimensions of AI literacy for education:

| Dimension | Description |.
|----------|-------------|
| **Understand** | Know how AI works at the conceptual level |
| **Use** |Use AI tools effectively for learning tasks |
| **Evaluate** | Critically evaluate AI outputs |
| **Create** | Using AI for creative and complex tasks |

## Distinction: automation vs augmentation

An important conceptual distinction in the literature (Selwyn, 2022; Dillenbourg, 2023) is that between:

- **Automation**: AI replaces human tasks (e.g. automatic review of multiple choice)
- **Augmentation**: AI enhances human capabilities (e.g. AI as a sparring partner in writing)

The vast majority of education literature argues for an augmentation model where the teacher and student remain central.
